About us

Berliner Bengalis (Berlin er bangali) is a group of Bengalis that started it's journey in 2023. Sanatani way of life is very close to our heart and we are deeply rooted to it's values and rituals.

Berlin is for us "home away from home". Berlin is a city that is very much known for it's multicultural existence. We as a sanatani group, would like to contribute to that with our cultural practices for us and the generations to come.

Alongside Sanatani practices, we would also focus on rituals around Bengali culture and most importantly something for the preservation of nature which we consider as "Mother Earth" as per tradition.

We strongly believe that, culture and social responsibilities go hand in hand. Practising Hindu Sanatani Culture through different activities and save the nature through creating awareness is our moto.

Durga Puja

Durga Puja is a major Hindu festival celebrated primrily among Sanatani Hindus in India and in other parts of the world with Bengali communities. The essence of Goddess Durga embodies strength, courage, and protection. She is believed to symbolize the divine feminine energy that can overcome and conquer any negative forces or challenges in life. Durga represents the power to stand up against injustice and to vanquish evil, ultimately restoring harmony and righteousness. Her story and attributes inspire devotion, resilience, and the belief in the triumph of good over evil. According to Hindu mythological practices, the worship of goddess Durga is being practiced twice in a year. Basanti Puja also known as Chaitra Durga Puja is celebrated during Indian Hindu calendar month of Chaitra, Bosonto Kaal or the spring season. And, Akaal Bodhan or Sarodiya Durga Puja is celebrated in the Indian Hindu calendar month of  Ashwin , which corresponds to September – October in the  Gregorian calendar . Basanti Puja is celebrated widely among Indian Sanatani Hindus but Akaal Bodhan or Sarodiya Durga Puja is practiced and celebrated in a much greater way.

Etymology of Akaal Bodhan

Both the words Akaal and Bodhan descendant from  Sanskrit word akālabodhana , which are also included in many other languages, such as  Bengali ,  Assamese ,  Odia  and other languages of  Bay of Bengal . The word Akaal means untimed (kaal=time and a=not) and the word Bodhan means worship or invocation. Thus, Akaal Bodhan means worship or invocation of  Durga  in an uncustomary time. It is given this name since the period of this worship differs from the conventional period, which is during the spring (Vasanta). There are many mythological believes and stories as per Sanatani Hindu religion behind the reason of celebration of Akaal Bodhan or Sarodiya Durga Puja.

The reference to Akaal Bodhan is found in the Mahabhagavata Purana. According to this Purana, when Ravana awakened Kumbhakarna and sent him to the war, Rama was terrified. Then Brahma assured Rama and told him to worship Goddess Durga (Mahabhagavat 41.22-33) for success on the battlefield. Rama told Brahma that this was not the proper time (Akaal) to worship the Goddess as this was Krishnapaksha and it is time for her sleep. Then Brahma said that on behalf of Rama, he will perform the Bodhana and the Puja. Rama agreed and appointed Brahma as the Purohita (priest) (Mahabhagavata 42.12-27). Brahma agreed to be his purohit and perform the puja from Krishna Navami till the death of the Ravana (Shukla Navami)(Mahabhagavata 43.81-97). Following the instruction of Brahma, Rama praised the Goddess by uttering Katyanai Stab (Mahabhagavata 44.1-17). Brahma himself uttered the Devi Sukta from the Vedas to please the Goddess (Mahabhagavata 45.4-19). Devi was pleased and she appeared and said that there will be a great war between Rama and Ravana between Shukla Saptami to Shukla Navami. On Saptami, she will enter into Rama's bow and arrows. During the time when Ashtami tithi will end and Navami will start in that juncture (Sandhi) she will cut Ravana's heads one after another. And finally, she assured that in the afternoon on Shukla Navami tithi, she will destroy Ravana completely (Mahabhagavata 45.20-43).

Durga Puja 2023

During Durga puja, elaborate and artistic idols of Goddess Durga are crafted and installed in beautifully decorated pandals (temporary structures). Devotees offer prayers, perform traditional rituals, and enjoy cultural performances, including music and dance. It's a time of joy, feasting, and a strong sense of community. The festival holds immense cultural and religious significance among Sanatani Hindus and is celebrated with great enthusiasm and grandeur, attracting devotees and tourists from all over the world. We, Berliner Bengalis remains connected with our Sanatani Hindu practices and will celebrate Durga Puja in 2023. We will conduct Durga Puja at Radha Govinda Temple, Berlin as per the schedule of Panjika from 20th October 2023 (Sasthi) till 24th October 2023 (Dashami). At the same time, we strongly believe that we need to save our mother nature so that, we can sustain in this universe. And, we are going to demonstrate this message in Berlin through the worship of Maa Durga. Our theme represents ´Save the Nature´. According to our theme ´Save the Nature´, the Idol of Maa Durga is represented. Since we are expecting an immense crowd during the festivities of Durga Puja 2023, we hope 'Save the Nature' concept will reach among people in and around Berlin, Germany.
