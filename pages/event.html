Mahalaya (28th September)

üìÖ Saturday, September 28
Opening Ritual

The auspicious beginning of Durga Puja begins with Mahalaya. This is the day when we invoke the Goddess's presence through Chandi Path recitation and sacred rituals. The atmosphere fills with devotion as we prepare the pandal for the divine celebrations ahead.

Highlights: Chandi Path, ritual preparations, blessing ceremony

Shashti (29th September)

üìÖ Sunday, September 29
Grand Opening

Shashti marks the official opening of the pandal! The beautifully decorated idol of Goddess Durga is revealed, and devotees get their first darshan of Maa Durga. This is an exciting day with the community gathering to honor the Goddess.

Highlights: Pandal opening, first darshan, welcoming ceremony, community gathering

3Ô∏è‚É£

Saptami (30th September)

üìÖ Monday, September 30
Major Worship

Durga Saptami is one of the most significant days of the festival. Major religious ceremonies and pujas are performed. The day is filled with spiritual energy as devotees come for prayers, and cultural programs showcase Bengali traditions.

Highlights: Major rituals, Kshatri Puja, cultural performances, music and dance

4Ô∏è‚É£

Ashtami & Sandhi (1st October)

üìÖ Tuesday, October 1
Sacred Days

Ashtami is the eighth day, a day of continued devotion and powerful rituals. The Sandhi Puja (junction ceremony) is performed with great reverence, marking the transition between Ashtami and Navami. Young girls (Kumari) are worshipped as manifestations of the Goddess.

Highlights: Kumari Puja, Sandhi Puja, evening celebrations, community meals

5Ô∏è‚É£

Navami (2nd October)

üìÖ Wednesday, October 2
Peak Celebration

Maha Navami is the climax of the festival! The day is filled with joy, celebrations, and community festivities. Cultural programs reach their peak with performances of Bengali classical music, traditional dance, theater, and more. It's a day of pure celebration and togetherness.

Highlights: Peak celebrations, grand cultural programs, music concerts, dance performances, community feasts

6Ô∏è‚É£

Dashami - Vijayadashami (3rd October)

üìÖ Friday, October 3
Grand Finale

Dashami, also known as Vijayadashami, is the Victory Day! This day celebrates the triumph of good over evil, of Goddess Durga over the demon Mahishasura. Sindur Khela (the playful exchange of vermillion) is a joyful ritual, followed by Bijaya Sammilani (farewell gathering) where we bid goodbye to the Goddess.

Highlights: Final prayers, Sindur Khela, Bijaya Sammilani, farewell celebration, community meal

Frequently Asked Questions

Is entry free?

Yes, entry to the pandal is free for everyone! We welcome all community members and friends. Any contributions or donations are voluntary and greatly appreciated to support the event.

What are the timings?

The pandal is generally open from morning through evening during the festival days. Specific timings for rituals and cultural programs will be shared on our invitations and social media. Check back for detailed schedules!

Can I bring my family and children?

Absolutely! This is a family-friendly celebration. Children of all ages are welcome. The festival is a wonderful opportunity to introduce cultural traditions to the next generation.

Is food available at the venue?

Yes! We will have authentic Bengali cuisine, traditional sweets, snacks, and refreshments available. You can enjoy delicious food while celebrating with us.

How can I help or volunteer?

We love community support! If you'd like to volunteer, contribute, or help organize, please reach out to us at info@berlinerbengalis.com

Can non-Bengali people attend?

Definitely! Our celebration is for everyone. We welcome people of all backgrounds, faiths, and cultures who wish to experience and celebrate Durga Puja with us. It's a wonderful opportunity to learn about Bengali traditions and Hindu culture.

Have Questions?

For any queries, sponsorship opportunities, or if you'd like to be part of our celebration, please don't hesitate to reach out.
