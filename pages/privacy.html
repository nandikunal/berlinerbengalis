1. Introduction

Berliner Bengalis e.V. ("we," "us," "our," or "Company") is committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring you have a positive experience on our website. This Privacy Policy explains our data protection practices in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and other applicable EU and German data protection laws.

This policy applies to all information collected through our website (https://berlinerbengalis.com) and related services.

3. Data We Collect

3.1 Information You Provide Directly

  • Contact Form Data: Name, email address, subject, and message content
  • Communication: Email communications, inquiries, and feedback
  • Event Registration: Name, email, phone number (if applicable) for event participation

3.2 Information Collected Automatically

  • Server Logs: IP address, browser type, operating system, referring URL, pages visited
  • Analytics Data: Website usage patterns, interaction metrics (via Google Analytics)
  • Cookies & Tracking: Session cookies, preference cookies, analytics tracking IDs
  • Device Information: Device type, screen resolution, language preferences

3.3 Data NOT Collected

  • Payment information (we do not process payments directly)
  • Sensitive personal data (race, religion, political opinions) unless explicitly provided for registration
  • Location data (except as inferred from IP address)
  • Biometric data

5. How We Use Your Data

  • Service Delivery: Respond to contact forms, answer inquiries, provide requested information
  • Website Improvement: Analyze user behavior to enhance user experience and functionality
  • Communication: Send newsletters (only with your explicit consent), event updates, and administrative notices
  • Analytics: Track website performance, visitor statistics, and usage patterns
  • Legal Compliance: Fulfill legal obligations and enforce our terms of service
  • Security: Protect against fraud, abuse, and unauthorized access
  • Marketing: Personalized advertising (only with explicit consent via CMP banner)
Important: We do NOT share your data with third parties for marketing purposes without your explicit consent, and we do NOT sell your personal data under any circumstances.

6. Cookies & Tracking Technologies

6.1 Cookie Types

  • Essential Cookies: Required for website functionality (e.g., session management)
  • Functional Cookies: Remember user preferences and settings
  • Analytics Cookies: Collect usage data for performance analysis (Google Analytics)
  • Marketing Cookies: Enable personalized advertising and tracking

6.2 Cookie Consent Management

Our website uses a Consent Management Platform (CMP) to manage cookie preferences. When you visit our site, you can:

  • Accept all cookies by clicking "Consent"
  • Manage individual cookie preferences by clicking "Manage"
  • Configure which types of cookies you allow
  • Withdraw consent at any time

6.3 Google Analytics

We use Google Analytics to collect anonymized website usage data. This data is subject to Google's Privacy Policy. You can opt-out of Google Analytics tracking by:

  • Using our CMP banner to disable Analytics cookies
  • Installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Extension

6.4 Third-Party Cookies

Third-party services on our website (Google AdSense, etc.) may place their own cookies. These are governed by their respective privacy policies.

7. Third-Party Services & Data Sharing

7.1 Services Used

  • Google Analytics: Website analytics (anonymized data)
  • Google AdSense: Personalized advertising display
  • Font Awesome: Icon library (content delivery only)
  • CDNs: Content delivery for performance optimization

7.2 Data Transfers to Third Parties

Your personal data is only shared with third parties when necessary for service delivery and only with appropriate data processing agreements in place. We use Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) and adequacy decisions for international data transfers.

7.3 No Data Selling

We absolutely do NOT sell, rent, lease, or trade your personal data to any third party. Data sharing is limited to what is necessary for service delivery.

9. Your Data Protection Rights (GDPR Articles 15-22)

Under GDPR, you have the following rights:

9.1 Right of Access (Article 15)

You have the right to request a copy of all personal data we hold about you in a structured, commonly-used format.

9.2 Right to Rectification (Article 16)

You can request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data.

9.3 Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten") (Article 17)

You can request deletion of your personal data, except where legal obligations require retention.

9.4 Right to Restrict Processing (Article 18)

You can request that we limit how we use your data pending resolution of disputes or your review.

9.5 Right to Data Portability (Article 20)

You can request your data in a portable, machine-readable format for transfer to another service provider.

9.6 Right to Object (Article 21)

You can object to processing for direct marketing, profiling, or automated decision-making.

9.7 Right to Withdraw Consent

You can withdraw consent for data processing at any time using our CMP banner or by contacting us.

9.8 How to Exercise Your Rights

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at:

Email: info@berlinerbengalis.com
Subject Line: "GDPR Data Subject Request"
Response Time: 30 days (extendable to 90 days for complex requests)

10. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction, including:

  • HTTPS encryption for data in transit
  • Secure server infrastructure
  • Access controls and authentication
  • Regular security audits and updates
  • Staff training on data protection

Note: While we take security seriously, no online system is 100% secure. Please notify us immediately of any suspected data breach.

11. Children's Data Protection

Our website is not intended for children under 16 years old. We do not knowingly collect data from children under 16. If we become aware that a child under 16 has provided us with personal data, we will delete it immediately and notify the parent/guardian.

Parental consent is required for children aged 13-16 to provide data in certain jurisdictions.

12. International Data Transfers

Since we operate within the EU/Germany, most data remains within EU territory. When data is transferred internationally, we ensure compliance with GDPR through:

  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
  • Adequacy decisions by the European Commission
  • Appropriate safeguards and impact assessments

13. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically to reflect changes in our practices, technology, legal requirements, or other factors. We will notify you of material changes by updating the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page and, where appropriate, by sending you a notification email.

Your continued use of the website following such changes constitutes your acceptance of the updated Privacy Policy.
